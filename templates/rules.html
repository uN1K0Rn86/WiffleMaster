Wiffleball is similar to baseball, but it is played with a plastic ball and plastic bats. Like in baseball, there are three bases. The bases are positioned closer to each other than in baseball, and the outfield is smaller. A ball which is hit beyond to back line is a home run, and balls hit to the left or right side of the field are foul balls.

Like in baseball, a hitter is out if the ball he hits is caught directly from the air. The ball can be caught in foul territory, but not in home run territory. If the ball hits the ground (in fair territory) before being caught, it has to be thrown to first base before the hitter gets there. The fielder can also throw the ball at the hitter. The hitter will be out if the ball hits him before he reaches the base.

A plate appearance (PA) is an instance of a pitcher throwing a set of pitches to a batter. If the pitch ends up in the strike zone or the batter swings and misses at the pitch, it is considered a strike. Otherwise, it is a ball. For statistical purposes, a pitch that is batted into play is considered a strike. The count is expressed in the form balls - strikes. If the count reaches four (4) balls, the batter gets a walk, allowing him to proceed to first base. If there is already a runner on first base, the runner moves to second. Similarly, a runner on second base moves to third base (but only if there is also a runner on first). If the count reaches three (3) strikes, the batter is out.

A wiffleball game consists of a number (usually 3) of innings. An inning consists of a top and bottom half. The away team always bats first (during the top of the inning) and the home team bats second. The inning is over when there are 3 outs. The inning also ends if a team scores 5 or more runs, unless said team is trailing by more than 5 runs. In that case, the inning continues until the game is tied or there are 3 outs.

The game ends once the predetermined number of innings is played, unless the game is tied at that point. In that case, extra innings are played until the game has a winner.


Abbreviations
Abbreviation Explanation
PA Plate appearance. Every set of pitches resulting in a walk, hit, or out is considered a plate appearance.
BB Short for base on balls. This is the same as a walk, i.e. the pitcher throws 4 balls and the batter goes to first base
IBB Intentional base on balls (intentional walk). The pitcher announces that he would rather walk the batter than pitch to him
AB At bat. The total amount of plate appearances minus the amount of walks.
H Hit. The batter hits the ball into play and reaches base safely.
Avg Batting average. The amount of hits divided by the amount of at bats.
OBP On base percentage. The amount of hits plus walks divided by the number of plate appearances
XBH Extra base hit. The batter hits the ball into play and reaches at least second base.
HR Home Run. The batter hits the ball and scores on the same play. Usually happens by hitting the ball beyond the back line.
TB Total bases. A single gives one base, a double gives 2, a triple gives 3, and a home run gives 4.
SLG Slugging percentage. The total number of bases divided by the number of at bats.
OPS On base percentage plus slugging. This advanced statistic is meant to better represent the batte's performance, as it takes into account both how often the batter reaches base, and the quality of his hits.
IP Innings pitched. The amount of innings pitched by a pitcher. Written in the form 'complete innings'.'additional outs'. Eg. 2.2 IP
ER Earned runs. The amount of runs a pitcher has given up.
ERA Earned run average. The average amount of earned runs the pitcher has given up per 9 innings pitched.
